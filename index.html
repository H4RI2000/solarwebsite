What is the Solar System?

The solar system consists of the Sun and everything bound to it by gravity. This includes the eight planets, their moons, dwarf planets, asteroids, comets, and other celestial bodies. The Sun, a massive star at the center, provides the energy necessary for life on Earth. The solar system is a dynamic and fascinating place, full of mysteries and wonders waiting to be explored.

Moons of Our Solar System

Moons come in many shapes, sizes, and types. A few have atmospheres and even hidden oceans beneath their surfaces. more details

EUROPA

Europa is one of Jupiter's largest moons and is believed to have a subsurface ocean beneath its icy crust.It has the smoothest surface of any known solid object in the solar system, which might indicate a water-ice mixture.

TITAN

Titan is Saturn's largest moon and has a thick atmosphere rich in nitrogen.It has lakes and rivers of liquid methane and ethane on its surface, making it one of the most Earth-like bodies in the solar system.

LUNA

Luna, commonly known as the Moon, is Earth's only natural satellite and the fifth largest moon in the solar system.Its surface is covered with vast plains, mountains, and craters, and it has a significant impact on Earth's tides.

ASTEROIDS

Asteroids are minor planets that orbit the inner Solar System, and are made of rock, metal, or ice. They are smaller than the major planets in our solar system and have no atmosphere.
